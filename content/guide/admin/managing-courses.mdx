# Creating & Managing Courses

<RoleTag role="admin" />

Courses are the core educational product of PATHS. Each course contains a structured hierarchy of modules and lessons that guide learners through longevity topics.

## Course Structure

<Screenshot src="admin/courses-list.png" alt="Courses collection list showing courses with titles, pillars, and status" caption="The Courses collection — each course contains modules and lessons in a structured hierarchy." />

Courses follow a three-level hierarchy:

- **Course** — The top-level container with a title, description, access level, and content pillar.
- **Modules** — Thematic sections within a course (e.g., "Fundamentals of Sleep Hygiene").
- **Lessons** — Individual learning units within a module, containing rich text content and optional video embeds.

## Creating a Course

<StepList>
<Step number={1}>Navigate to **Courses** in the sidebar and click **Create New**.</Step>
<Step number={2}>Enter the course **title** and **description**.</Step>
<Step number={3}>Select a **content pillar** to categorize the course.</Step>
<Step number={4}>Set the **access level** to control which membership tiers can access the course.</Step>
<Step number={5}>Assign the course to a **tenant** (required for multi-tenancy).</Step>
<Step number={6}>Click **Save** to create the course shell.</Step>
</StepList>

## Adding Modules

<Screenshot src="admin/module-edit-form.png" alt="Module edit form showing title, parent course relationship, and order fields" caption="Modules link to a parent course and are ordered by the numeric order field." />

<StepList>
<Step number={1}>Navigate to **Modules** in the sidebar and click **Create New**.</Step>
<Step number={2}>Enter the module **title** and **description**.</Step>
<Step number={3}>Select the parent **course** from the relationship dropdown.</Step>
<Step number={4}>Set the **order** number to control the module's position within the course (lower numbers appear first).</Step>
<Step number={5}>Click **Save**.</Step>
</StepList>

## Adding Lessons

<StepList>
<Step number={1}>Navigate to **Lessons** in the sidebar and click **Create New**.</Step>
<Step number={2}>Enter the lesson **title**.</Step>
<Step number={3}>Select the parent **module** from the relationship dropdown.</Step>
<Step number={4}>Set the **order** number to position the lesson within its module.</Step>
<Step number={5}>Write the lesson content using the Lexical rich text editor. You can add text, headings, images, callout blocks, and quiz questions.</Step>
<Step number={6}>To embed a video, use the **Video Embed** block and paste a YouTube or Vimeo URL. PATHS does not host videos directly.</Step>
<Step number={7}>Click **Save**.</Step>
</StepList>

<Screenshot src="admin/lesson-lexical-editor.png" alt="Lesson editor showing Lexical rich text with video embed and quiz blocks" caption="The Lexical editor supports rich text, video embeds, callouts, and quiz question blocks." />

<Callout type="info" title="No direct video hosting">
PATHS uses YouTube and Vimeo embeds only. Upload your videos to either platform and paste the embed URL into the Video Embed block.
</Callout>

## Ordering Content

Module and lesson order is controlled by the numeric **order** field. Items with lower order numbers appear first. If two items share the same order number, their display order may be unpredictable — assign unique order values.

## Publishing Courses

<Accordion>
<AccordionItem title="Editorial workflow stages">
Courses follow the same editorial workflow as articles:

1. **Draft** — Initial creation, visible only to admins and the creator.
2. **In Review** — Submitted for editorial review.
3. **Approved** — Reviewed and approved by an editor.
4. **Published** — Live and visible to learners with sufficient access level.
</AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

<Callout type="warning" title="Access level gating">
Even after publishing, a course is only visible to users whose membership tier meets or exceeds the course's access level. A premium course (access level 3) is invisible to free-tier users.
</Callout>
