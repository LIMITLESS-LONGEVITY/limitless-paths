# Reviewing Content

<RoleTag role="editor" />

When a contributor finishes drafting an article, they submit it for review by changing its status to **In Review**. As an Editor, your job is to evaluate these submissions for quality, accuracy, and readiness.

## Finding Articles to Review

<StepList>
<Step number={1}>Open the **Articles** collection from the admin sidebar.</Step>
<Step number={2}>Click the **Status** column header or use the filter controls to filter by **In Review**.</Step>
<Step number={3}>Articles are listed with their last-modified date — prioritize older submissions to keep the pipeline moving.</Step>
</StepList>

<Screenshot src="editor/reviewing-content-queue.png" alt="Articles list filtered by In Review status showing submissions sorted by last-modified date" />

<Callout type="tip" title="Check the queue regularly">
Contributors are waiting on your feedback. Aim to review submitted articles within 48 hours to maintain momentum and contributor engagement.
</Callout>

## What to Look For

When reviewing an article, assess it across these dimensions:

- **Accuracy** — Are the claims factually correct? Are sources or references appropriate for the topic?
- **Clarity** — Is the writing clear and accessible to the target audience? Are complex concepts explained well?
- **Structure** — Does the article flow logically? Are headings, sections, and paragraphs well-organized?
- **Formatting** — Are rich text elements (lists, callouts, embedded media) used correctly? Do images have alt text?
- **Completeness** — Does the article cover its topic adequately? Are there obvious gaps?

## Reviewing the Article

<StepList>
<Step number={1}>Click into the article to open the full editor view.</Step>
<Step number={2}>Read through the content in the rich text editor. You can preview how it will appear on the frontend by using the preview function if available.</Step>
<Step number={3}>Check metadata fields — title, description, category, and tags should all be filled in appropriately.</Step>
<Step number={4}>If the article includes quiz questions, verify that correct answers are marked and explanations are provided.</Step>
</StepList>

<Screenshot src="editor/reviewing-content-editor.png" alt="Article open in the admin editor showing content body, metadata fields, and status controls" />

<Callout type="info">
You can make minor formatting fixes directly in the editor. For substantive changes, send the article back to the contributor with notes rather than rewriting their content.
</Callout>

## After Your Review

Once you have assessed the article, you have two paths forward:

- **Approve it** — If the article meets standards, move it to **Approved**. See [Approving Content](/guide/editor/approving-content).
- **Request changes** — If improvements are needed, move it back to **Draft** with feedback. See [Working with Contributors](/guide/editor/working-with-contributors).
