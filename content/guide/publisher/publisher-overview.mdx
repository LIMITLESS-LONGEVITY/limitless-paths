# Publisher Role Overview

<RoleTag role="publisher" />

As a Publisher on the PATHS platform, you control what learners see. You are responsible for taking editorially approved content live, maintaining the quality of published material, and archiving content that is no longer current.

## Your Responsibilities

- **Publish approved content** — Move articles that Editors have approved to **Published** status, making them visible to learners on the frontend.
- **Manage live content** — Monitor published articles and ensure they remain accurate and relevant over time.
- **Archive outdated material** — Remove content that is no longer current from active listings while preserving URLs for existing links.
- **Coordinate timing** — Work with the editorial team to manage when content goes live, aligning with organizational priorities.

## Accessing the Admin Panel

<StepList>
<Step number={1}>Log in to the PATHS admin panel at your organization's admin URL.</Step>
<Step number={2}>Navigate to **Articles** in the left sidebar.</Step>
<Step number={3}>Filter by **Approved** status to see content that is ready for publication.</Step>
</StepList>

<Screenshot src="publisher/publisher-overview-queue.png" alt="Publisher view of the Articles collection filtered by Approved status showing content ready for publication" />

<Callout type="info" title="What you can and cannot do">
Publishers can transition articles from **Approved** to **Published** and from **Published** to **Archived**. You cannot edit article content directly — if you spot an issue in an approved article, send it back to the editorial team for correction before publishing.
</Callout>

## The Publishing Pipeline

Content arrives in your queue after passing through the editorial process. By the time an article reaches **Approved** status, it has been written by a contributor and reviewed by an Editor. Your role is the final step before learners see the content.
