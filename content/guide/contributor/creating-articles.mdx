# Creating a New Article

<RoleTag role="contributor" />

This guide walks you through every step of creating a new article in PATHS, from navigating to the Articles collection to saving your first draft.

## Before You Begin

Make sure you are logged into the Payload admin panel with your contributor account. You should see **Articles** listed in the left sidebar navigation.

<Callout type="info">
If you do not see the Articles collection, contact your administrator to verify your role and permissions.
</Callout>

## Step-by-Step: Create an Article

<StepList>
<Step number={1}>Navigate to the admin panel and click **Articles** in the left sidebar.</Step>
<Step number={2}>Click the **Create New** button in the top-right corner of the Articles list view.</Step>

<Screenshot src="contributor/creating-articles-list.png" alt="Articles list view with the Create New button highlighted in the top-right corner" />
<Step number={3}>Enter a descriptive **Title** for your article. Choose something clear and specific — this is what learners will see in search results and content listings.</Step>
<Step number={4}>The **Slug** field auto-generates from your title. You can edit it manually if needed, but keep it URL-friendly (lowercase, hyphens, no spaces).</Step>
<Step number={5}>Select a **Content Pillar** from the dropdown. Every article must belong to one of the six longevity pillars: Nutrition, Exercise, Sleep, Stress Management, Supplements, or Diagnostics.</Step>
<Step number={6}>Set the **Access Level** to determine which membership tiers can view this article. Choose from Free, Essential, Professional, or Premium.</Step>
<Step number={7}>Upload a **Featured Image** by clicking the image field and selecting or uploading a file from the Media library. This image appears in article cards and at the top of the article page.</Step>
<Step number={8}>Write your article content in the **Lexical rich text editor**. Use headings, paragraphs, lists, and blocks to structure your content. See the [Lexical Editor guide](/guide/contributor/lexical-editor) for detailed formatting instructions.</Step>
<Step number={9}>Leave the **Status** field set to **Draft**. Do not change this until you are ready to submit for review.</Step>

<Screenshot src="contributor/creating-articles-form.png" alt="Article edit form showing title, slug, content pillar, access level, and status fields" />
<Step number={10}>Click **Save** to save your article as a draft. You can return to edit it at any time.</Step>
</StepList>

## Required Fields

<Accordion title="Fields required before submission">
Every article must have the following fields completed before it can be submitted for review:

- **Title** — a clear, descriptive headline
- **Content Pillar** — the longevity category this article belongs to
- **Access Level** — who can read this article
- **Content** — the article body, written in the Lexical editor
</Accordion>

<Callout type="warning" title="Featured image matters">
While technically optional, articles without a featured image look incomplete in content listings. Always upload a relevant, high-quality image.
</Callout>

## Saving and Returning

Your article is saved as a draft automatically when you click **Save**. You can close the browser and return later — find your drafts by navigating to **Articles** and filtering by status. See [Managing Your Drafts](/guide/contributor/managing-drafts) for tips on organizing your work.

<Callout type="tip">
Save your work frequently as you write. The editor does not auto-save, so use the Save button regularly to avoid losing progress.
</Callout>
