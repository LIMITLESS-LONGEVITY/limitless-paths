# Expert Profiles

<RoleTag role="contributor" />

Every article and course on PATHS prominently features the expert who created it. As a contributor, you can set up your **Expert Profile** so readers see your credentials, specialization, and professional background alongside your content.

## Why Expert Profiles Matter

Readers on PATHS are executives and high-performers who want to know they're learning from real practitioners — not anonymous content creators. Your expert profile builds trust and differentiates PATHS content from generic education platforms.

## Setting Up Your Profile

Your expert profile fields are part of your user account in the admin panel.

<StepList>
  <Step number={1}>Log in to the **Admin Panel** at `/admin`.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Click your avatar in the top right and select **Account**.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Scroll down to the **Expert Profile** section.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Fill in the following fields:
    - **Bio** — A short professional biography (2-3 sentences)
    - **Expertise** — Your area of specialization (e.g., "Metabolic Medicine," "Sleep Science")
    - **Credentials** — Your qualifications (e.g., "MD, PhD — Head of Metabolic Medicine, Recoletas Salud")
    - **LinkedIn URL** — Link to your professional LinkedIn profile
    - **Public Profile** — Toggle on to make your profile visible to readers
  </Step>
  <Step number={5}>Click **Save** to update your profile.</Step>
</StepList>

<Screenshot src="contributor/expert-profile-fields.png" alt="Expert profile fields in the admin account page" caption="Fill in your bio, expertise, credentials, and LinkedIn URL." />

## How Your Profile Appears

When your profile is set to **Public**, an **ExpertCard** appears on every article and course you author:

- **On articles:** Below the title, showing your avatar, name, credentials, and a LinkedIn link
- **On courses:** In the instructor section, with your full bio and expertise area
