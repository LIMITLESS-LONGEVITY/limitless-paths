# Adding Media, Videos & Callouts

<RoleTag role="contributor" />

The Lexical editor includes six custom block types that make your articles more engaging and interactive. To insert any block, place your cursor at the desired location and click the **"+" button** in the toolbar.

## Video Embed

Embed videos from YouTube or Vimeo directly into your article. PATHS does not host video files — all video content is delivered via third-party embeds.

<StepList>
<Step number={1}>Click the "+" button and select **Video Embed**.</Step>
<Step number={2}>Paste the full YouTube or Vimeo URL into the URL field (e.g., `https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=...`).</Step>
<Step number={3}>Optionally add a caption to describe the video content.</Step>
</StepList>

<Screenshot src="contributor/media-and-blocks-video.png" alt="Video Embed block in the Lexical editor with URL field and caption input" />

<Callout type="tip">
Use the standard watch URL for YouTube videos, not the shortened `youtu.be` format. The embed block will extract the video ID automatically.
</Callout>

## Quiz Question

Add multiple-choice knowledge checks directly within your article. Quizzes help reinforce key concepts and keep learners actively engaged.

<StepList>
<Step number={1}>Click the "+" button and select **Quiz Question**.</Step>
<Step number={2}>Enter the **question text** in the question field.</Step>
<Step number={3}>Add at least two **answer options**. Click "Add Option" to add more choices.</Step>
<Step number={4}>Mark the **correct answer** by selecting the radio button next to it.</Step>
<Step number={5}>Optionally add an **explanation** that displays after the learner answers, explaining why the correct answer is right.</Step>

<Screenshot src="contributor/media-and-blocks-quiz.png" alt="Quiz Question block showing question text, answer options with correct answer radio button, and explanation field" />
</StepList>

<Callout type="info" title="Quiz best practices">
Write clear, unambiguous questions with one definitively correct answer. Avoid trick questions. Include 3-4 options for the best balance of challenge and usability.
</Callout>

## Callout

Callouts are styled boxes that draw attention to important information. Four types are available:

- **Info** — general supplementary information or context
- **Warning** — cautions, prerequisites, or potential pitfalls
- **Tip** — helpful suggestions and best practices
- **Success** — positive outcomes, confirmations, or achievements

<StepList>
<Step number={1}>Click the "+" button and select **Callout**.</Step>
<Step number={2}>Choose the **callout type** (info, warning, tip, or success).</Step>
<Step number={3}>Write the callout content in the text area. You can use basic formatting inside callouts.</Step>
</StepList>

<Callout type="warning">
Do not overuse callouts. If everything is highlighted, nothing stands out. Reserve callouts for genuinely important points that learners should not miss.
</Callout>

<Accordion title="Audio, Gallery & PDF blocks">
## Audio Embed

Embed podcast clips or narrated explanations. Select **Audio Embed** from the "+" menu, upload a file or provide a URL, and add a title.

## Image Gallery

Display multiple images in a gallery layout. Select **Image Gallery**, upload images or choose from the Media library, and add captions.

## PDF Viewer

Embed PDF documents for viewing or download. Select **PDF Viewer**, upload a PDF or choose from the Media library, and add a title.
</Accordion>

<Callout type="tip" title="Choosing the right block">
Use video embeds for demonstrations, quiz questions after teaching a concept, callouts for critical reminders, and image galleries for visual evidence or processes.
</Callout>
