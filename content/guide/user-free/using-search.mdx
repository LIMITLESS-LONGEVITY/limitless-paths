# Using Search

<RoleTag role="user-free" />

PATHS includes an AI-powered semantic search that goes beyond simple keyword matching. Instead of requiring exact terms, you can describe what you are looking for in natural language, and the search engine will find the most relevant results.

The search page features an **AI-Powered** badge to indicate that results are ranked by semantic relevance, not just keyword matches. Each result also shows a **type label** (Article, Course, or Lesson) so you know what kind of content you're navigating to.

<Screenshot src="user-free/search-ai-badge.png" alt="PATHS search page with AI-Powered badge" caption="The PATHS search page with AI-Powered badge and semantic search." />

## How Semantic Search Works

Traditional search matches keywords in titles and text. PATHS search uses **AI embeddings** — a technology that understands the meaning behind your query. This means:

- Searching for "how to sleep better" will surface articles about sleep hygiene, circadian rhythms, and recovery protocols, even if those exact words don't appear in the content.
- You can ask questions naturally, like "what supplements support mitochondrial function" and get meaningful results.

<Callout type="info">
  Semantic search is available to all users, including free accounts. It searches across articles and courses that are visible at your access level.
</Callout>

## Performing a Search

<StepList>
  <Step number={1}>
    Click the **search icon** in the navigation bar or navigate to the search page. You can also press <KeyboardShortcut keys={['Ctrl', 'K']} mac={['Cmd', 'K']} /> to open search quickly.
  </Step>
  <Step number={2}>
    Type your query into the search field. You can use keywords, phrases, or full questions.
  </Step>
  <Step number={3}>
    Press **Enter** or click the search button to submit your query.
  </Step>
  <Step number={4}>
    Review the results, which are ranked by relevance. Each result shows the content title, a brief excerpt, and its pillar tag.

    <Screenshot src="user-free/search-results.png" alt="Search results page showing ranked results with pillar tags" caption="Search results ranked by semantic relevance" />
  </Step>
</StepList>

## Tips for Effective Searches

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="Search tips and techniques">
    - **Be specific** — "glucose regulation after meals" will return more focused results than just "nutrition."
    - **Use natural language** — The search engine understands intent, so phrasing your query as a question often works well.
    - **Try different angles** — If your first query doesn't surface what you need, rephrase it. For example, try "VO2 max training" instead of "cardiovascular fitness."
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

## Filtering Results

After performing a search, you can filter results by content type or pillar to narrow down the list. This is especially useful when a broad query returns results across multiple longevity domains.

<Callout type="warning" title="Access restrictions apply">
  Search results may include content from higher tiers. You will see the title and excerpt, but full access to locked content requires an upgraded plan.
</Callout>
